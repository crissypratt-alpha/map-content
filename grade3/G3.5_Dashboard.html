CRITICAL: Weakest Test in Grade 3 Suite

G3.5 has 77% DOK1 items — 24 of 31 questions are Scripted recall, the highest proportion of any G3 test. With an average complexity of 1.46 (the lowest of any G3 assessment, worse than G3.3's 1.63), nearly every question operates at the recall level. Only 2 questions (Q11, Q27) meet assessment quality standards. This test requires the most extensive revision of any G3 assessment.

Vocabulary Quiz Format — Not a Science Assessment

Questions Q1, Q2, Q3, Q4, Q5, Q7, Q8, and Q9 are simple sentence-completion or vocabulary definition tasks (e.g., "What is weather?", "What causes an object to move?"). The test reads more like a vocabulary quiz than a science assessment. Eight of the first nine questions are pure recall definitions with no scenario, no data, and no reasoning required.

What Do the Complexity Levels Mean?

From Achieve's Framework to Evaluate the Cognitive Complexity of Science Assessment Tasks (2019).

Scripted (Recall)
Student recalls a fact or definition. No reasoning. DOK Level 1. Target: 0%.
Low Guided (Application)
Student applies a concept to a familiar scenario. Some reasoning. Target: 40-50%.
High Guided (Transfer)
Student applies understanding to unfamiliar situations with data/evidence. Target: 20-30%.
Doing Science (Authentic)
Genuine scientific reasoning with novel data. No single right path. Target: 5-10%.

Content Coverage Flags

Issues with construct redundancy, topic balance, and question quality
  • CRITICAL: Scripted Overload (77% vs. 20-30% target)
    24 of 31 questions are Scripted recall — nearly 4x the upper target of 30%. This is the highest Scripted proportion of any G3 test and renders the assessment unable to meaningfully measure scientific proficiency.
  • CRITICAL: Vocabulary Quiz Format — Q1-Q5 and Q7-Q9
    Eight questions are simple definitions or sentence completions ("What is weather?", "What causes an object to move?", "Complete the sentence: Climate is the —"). The test reads more like a vocabulary quiz than a science assessment.
  • LS Overrepresentation (55% vs. 44% target)
    17 of 31 questions are Life Science, exceeding the NGSS target by 11 percentage points. Reduce LS items to make room for underrepresented domains.
  • ETS Underrepresentation (3% vs. 17% target)
    Only 1 ETS question on the entire test. The NGSS target is 17%. Use freed LS slots to add engineering design items with trade-off analysis.
  • Multiple Identical Questions Shared with Other Tests
    Q26 is identical to G3.4 Q28 (blizzard dangers) and Q28 is identical to G3.10 Q11 (mixture separation with magnet). Shared items compromise test security and validity.
  • Only 2 Strong Questions (Q11, Q27) — Tied for Fewest of Any G3 Test
    Q11 (Arctic vs. Tropical climate graph comparison) and Q27 (mouse camouflage natural selection) are the only items that meet assessment quality standards. Use as models for upgrading recall items.
  • Q29 Missing from PDF Extraction
    Q29 is absent from the extracted test. The test may have 32 questions total. This gap should be verified against the original assessment.

Q1 — Weather Definition (Scripted → High Guided)
Replace vocabulary recall with evidence-based analysis.
Before: "What is weather?"
After: "A student records today's conditions: 72°F, partly cloudy, 30% chance of rain. Their teacher shows a table of the same city's average conditions over 30 years [data table]. Which data shows weather and which shows climate? Explain using evidence."

Replace recall with data analysis.
