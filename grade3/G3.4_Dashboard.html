What Do the Complexity Levels Mean?

From Achieve's Framework to Evaluate the Cognitive Complexity of Science Assessment Tasks (2019).

Scripted (Recall)
Student recalls a fact or definition. No reasoning. DOK Level 1. Target: 0%.
Low Guided (Application)
Student applies a concept to a familiar scenario. Some reasoning. Target: 40-50%.
High Guided (Transfer)
Student applies understanding to unfamiliar situations with data/evidence. Target: 20-30%.
Doing Science (Authentic)
Genuine scientific reasoning with novel data. No single right path. Target: 5-10%.

Content Coverage Flags

Issues with construct redundancy and topic balance
  • LS Overrepresentation (56% vs. 44% target)
    18 of 32 questions are Life Science, exceeding the NGSS target by 12 percentage points. Reduce LS items to make room for underrepresented domains.
  • ETS Underrepresentation (3% vs. 17% target)
    Only 1 ETS question (Q28). This is the most severe domain gap in G3.4. Use freed LS slots to add engineering design items with trade-off analysis.
  • ESS Underrepresentation (13% vs. 17% target)
    Only 4 ESS questions (Q1, Q29, Q31, Q32). The Sydney climate graph (Q1) is Strong, but the other three are Scripted recall.
  • Quality Cliff After Q17
    Questions 18–32 are almost entirely Scripted recall. The last 15 questions dramatically reduce overall quality, creating a stark divide between the evidence-rich first half and the recall-heavy second half.
  • Strong Plant Exchange Cluster (Q2–Q3)
    Best evidence-based reasoning pair in any G3 test. The controlled plant exchange between Louisiana wetlands and Arizona desert provides rich experimental context for argument from evidence.
  • Tundra Animals Passage Cluster (Q7–Q9)
    Shows effective use of informational text for evidence-based reasoning. Q8 is Strong; Q7 and Q9 are Low Guided with potential for elevation.

Q12 — Pollination Agents (Scripted → High Guided)
Replace recall with experimental data analysis.
Before: "Which is NOT capable of pollinating?"
After: "Students tested which agents transfer pollen by placing flowers near wind, water, and insects [data table]. Based on the results, which agent was most effective? Which was least effective? Use data to support your answer."
