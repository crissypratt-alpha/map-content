What Do the Complexity Levels Mean?

From Achieve's Framework to Evaluate the Cognitive Complexity of Science Assessment Tasks (2019).

Scripted (Recall)
Student recalls a fact or definition. No reasoning. DOK Level 1. Target: 0%.
Low Guided (Application)
Student applies a concept to a familiar scenario. Some reasoning. Target: 40-50%.
High Guided (Transfer)
Student applies understanding to unfamiliar situations with data/evidence. Target: 20-30%.
Doing Science (Authentic)
Genuine scientific reasoning with novel data. No single right path. Target: 5-10%.

Content Coverage Flags

Issues with domain balance, question overlap, and standard alignment
  • LS Overrepresentation (55% vs. 44% target)
    17 of 31 questions are Life Science, exceeding the NGSS target by 11 percentage points. Reduce LS items to make room for underrepresented domains.
  • ETS Underrepresentation (3% vs. 17% target)
    Only 1 ETS question (Q13). This is the most underrepresented domain. Use freed LS slots to add engineering design items with trade-off analysis.
  • Heavy Question Overlap with G3.17 (Q13-Q23 Nearly Identical)
    A large block of questions (Q13-Q23) appears nearly identical to G3.17, creating test security and construct validity concerns. These items should be replaced or substantially revised to ensure independent measurement.
  • Beaver Passage Cluster (Q30-Q31) Is Excellent — Multi-Evidence Synthesis
    The beaver passage questions require students to synthesize multiple text details about adaptations, habitat requirements, and survival functions. Q30 and Q31 together demonstrate best-practice passage-based assessment. Use as a model for upgrading recall items.
  • Q26 (Mirror Reflectiveness) Is Off-Standard
    Reflectiveness is not a core Grade 3 NGSS topic. This question does not align with 3-PS2 standards on forces and interactions. Replace with an NGSS-aligned force or motion question with data.
