Achieve Standard: 0% DOK1 Items

This assessment has 55% DOK1 items — 12 of 22 questions are Scripted recall, the highest proportion among G3 tests. The Low Guided distribution (36%) falls short of the 40-50% target, and the test lacks any Doing Science items. Only 2 questions reach High Guided (Q12 using Cincinnati weather data and Q13 using flood prevention engineering with a data table). The test opens with three consecutive LS1-1 recall items (Q1-Q3), all targeting life cycle sequence knowledge. Life Science dominates at 64%, far exceeding the 44% NGSS target.

What Do the Complexity Levels Mean?

From Achieve's Framework to Evaluate the Cognitive Complexity of Science Assessment Tasks (2019).

Scripted (Recall)
Student recalls a fact or definition. No reasoning. DOK Level 1. Target: 0%.
Low Guided (Application)
Student applies a concept to a familiar scenario. Some reasoning. Target: 40-50%.
High Guided (Transfer)
Student applies understanding to unfamiliar situations with data/evidence. Target: 20-30%.
Doing Science (Authentic)
Genuine scientific reasoning with novel data. No single right path. Target: 5-10%.

Content Coverage Flags

Issues with construct redundancy and topic balance
  • LS Overrepresentation (64% vs. 44% target)
    14 of 22 questions are Life Science — the highest LS proportion of any G3 test, exceeding the NGSS target by 20 percentage points. Reduce LS items to make room for underrepresented domains.
  • ESS Underrepresentation (9% vs. 17% target)
    Only 2 ESS questions (Q11, Q12). Q12 is one of the two Strong items using Cincinnati weather data, but the domain needs more coverage.
  • ETS Underrepresentation (5% vs. 17% target)
    Only 1 ETS question (Q13), though it is a Strong item. Use freed LS slots to add engineering design items with trade-off analysis. Q20 (hurricane shutters) also touches engineering but is classified as Low Guided.
  • Three Consecutive LS1-1 Recall Items at Start (Q1, Q2, Q3)
    The test opens with three back-to-back life cycle sequence questions, all targeting the same standard (3-LS1-1) and all Scripted. Consolidate into one data-driven life cycle question to reduce redundancy.
  • Q12 (Cincinnati Weather Analysis) and Q13 (Flood Prevention Engineering) Are the Only Strong Items
    These two questions demonstrate effective use of real-world data and multi-criteria reasoning. Q12 requires evaluating claims against complex weather data; Q13 requires systematic evaluation of solutions against four constraints. Use as models for upgrading recall items.
  • Q9 (Camel Adaptation Meta-Question) Is an Interesting Metacognitive Item
    Q9 asks students to evaluate whether a paragraph qualifies as an adaptation explanation — a metacognitive task about what counts as scientific reasoning. While scored Low Guided, this format has potential for elevation with comparative data.

MAP Alignment Recommendations — Before & After

Q1 — Butterfly Life Cycle Order (Scripted → High Guided)
Replace sequence recall with evidence-based analysis.
Before: "Which list shows the correct order of the life-cycle stages for a butterfly?"
After: "A student observed changes in an unfamiliar insect over 8 weeks and recorded observations [data table showing dates, physical descriptions, and behaviors]. Based on the data, identify the life cycle stages and explain what evidence shows each transition."
Scripted → High Guided

Q2 — Common Life Cycle Stages (Scripted → High Guided)
Replace recall with comparative data analysis.
Before: "Which correctly represents the common life cycle stages shared by most animals regardless of species?"
After: "Scientists tracked the life stages of four animals: a frog, a chicken, a dog, and a butterfly [data table with stage durations and descriptions]. What stages do all four share? Which animal's life cycle is most different? Use data to support your answer."
Scripted → High Guided

Q3 — Orange Tree and Rose Bush (Scripted → High Guided)
Replace recall with comparative growth data.
Before: "How are the life cycles of an orange tree and a rose bush similar?"
After: "Here is growth data for three different plants over 12 months [data table/graph showing seed, seedling, flowering, and fruiting stages with timelines]. Compare the life cycles. What stages are common to all three? How do the timelines differ?"
Scripted → High Guided

Q7 — Caribou Group Survival (Scripted → High Guided)

After: "Scientists tracked caribou survival rates: solo caribou had 60% winter survival while herd caribou had 95% survival [data table with season, group size, and survival rate]. What does this data tell you about the benefit of living in herds? What pattern do you see across seasons?"
Scripted → High Guided
